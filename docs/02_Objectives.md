# 2. Project Objectives

## 2.1. Academic Objectives

- **Apply Database Theory:** To apply fundamental database concepts (normalization, constraints, recovery) in the evolving context of food supply chains (Mapping to Complex Engineering Activity **EA5: Familiarity**).

- **Demonstrate SQL Proficiency:** To successfully implement and execute complex queries including 10 single-table, 5 multi-table (JOINs), and all types of subqueries.

- **Implement Procedural Logic:** To utilize PostgreSQL's PL/pgSQL extension to write Functions, Stored Procedures, and Triggers that automate data integrity and business rules.

- **Achieve Normalization:** To design a relational schema strictly adhering to the Third Normal Form (3NF) to eliminate data redundancy and insertion/deletion anomalies (Mapping to **P3, P4**).

## 2.2. Functional Objectives

- **End-to-End Traceability:** To track a food batch from its origin (Producer) through various transit hubs (Logistics) to the end consumer point (Retailer).

- **Automated Safety Compliance:** To ensure food products remain within safe environmental conditions (temperature) and automatically quarantine items that fail inspections.

- **Chain of Custody Tracking:** To maintain an immutable log of which stakeholder had physical possession of a batch at any given time.
